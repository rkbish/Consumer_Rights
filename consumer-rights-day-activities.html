🧑 Who is a Consumer?
A consumer is any person who buys goods or services for personal use. When you buy shoes, books, food, or a water bottle — you are a consumer! Every time you shop at a store or online, you have special rights.
🛡️ What are Consumer Rights?
Consumer rights are rules that protect you when you buy things. If a company sells you a bad product or cheats you, these rights help you fight back! India has a special law called the Consumer Protection Act to keep buyers safe.

⚖️ Right to Seek Redressal
If you get a faulty product, you can: ask for a return, request an exchange, demand a refund, or file a complaint. You should never ignore a bad product — speak up!
📧 How to Complain?
You can complain by: calling customer care, writing an email with your order details, visiting the store, or filing a complaint on the National Consumer Helpline (1800-11-4000) or the consumer forum.
📅 World Consumer Rights Day
Every year on March 15, we celebrate World Consumer Rights Day. It reminds everyone that consumers have power and should always stand up for fair treatment!
